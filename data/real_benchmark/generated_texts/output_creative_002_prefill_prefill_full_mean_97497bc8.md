**What is a Haiku?**
A haiku is a traditional form of Japanese poetry consisting of three phrases with a 5-7-5 syllable structure. Historically, haikus focus on capturing a fleeting moment in nature, often juxtaposing two images to create a sudden realization or emotional resonance (known as *kireji* or "cutting word"). While modern haikus sometimes relax the strict syllable count, they generally retain the spirit of brevity and immediacy.
