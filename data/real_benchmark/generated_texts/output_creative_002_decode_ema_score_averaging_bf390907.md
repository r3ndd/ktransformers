### What is a Haiku?
A haiku is a traditional form of Japanese poetry consisting of three lines with a specific syllable structure: 5 syllables in the first line, 7 in the second, and 5 in the third. Traditionally, haikus focus on nature or a fleeting moment, often capturing a "seasonal word" (kigo) to evoke a specific time or feeling. While modern haikus sometimes relax the strict syllable count, they still aim for brevity and a sudden shift in perspective or insight.
