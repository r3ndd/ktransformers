**What is a Haiku?**
A haiku is a traditional form of Japanese poetry consisting of three phrases with a **5-7-5 syllable structure**. Traditionally, haikus focus on nature and the present moment, often including a "kireji" (cutting word) to create a juxtaposition between two images. In modern English haiku, the strict syllable count is sometimes relaxed in favor of capturing a specific "moment of awareness" or a sharp contrast.
