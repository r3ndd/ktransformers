**What is a Haiku?**
A haiku is a traditional form of Japanese poetry consisting of three phrases with a 5-7-5 syllable structure. Traditionally, it focuses on nature and the passage of time, often capturing a fleeting moment or a sudden realization (known as *kireji* or "cutting word"). In modern contexts, while the strict syllable count is sometimes relaxed for English speakers, the core essence remains: brevity, juxtaposition of images, and a focus on the immediate experience.
